# Secrets

## I already have a list

Seven names. Seven people or organizations with the means, motive, and capability to do something to a Martian corvette-class warship and leave no trace. I've been narrowing it down since day three. I cross-reference every piece of intelligence I can get my hands on — Avasarala's leaks, Belter gossip, Tycho traffic data, Martian military channels I technically no longer have access to but whose encryption I technically still know how to bypass.

Nobody asked me to investigate. Nobody assigned me this mission. I assigned myself. Because someone did this, and when I find out who, I am going to be standing in front of them. And they are going to understand what it means to have taken something from Bobbie Draper.

This isn't revenge. Revenge is sloppy. This is accountability.

## I wasn't supposed to be on shore leave

Holden didn't order me off the ship. I volunteered. I told him I needed a break because it was easier than the truth: I'd gotten a message from a contact on Mars. Old MMC intelligence — someone I'd served with who still owed me. They had information about protomolecule weapons research that hadn't been shut down after the war. A facility, still operating, hidden in the Mariner Valley bureaucracy.

I went to Tycho to meet the contact and follow up. I was going to tell Holden after I had something solid, because Holden hearing "secret protomolecule weapons lab" with incomplete intel is how you get a system-wide broadcast at 0300.

The meeting was scheduled for the morning after The Incident. My contact went dark. The facility — if it existed — has had six weeks to relocate.

I don't know if it's connected. I don't know if it's coincidence. I don't believe in coincidence.

## I'm afraid I failed them by not being there

Every scenario I run, I put myself back on the ship. Where would I have been? What would I have done? If it was a boarding action, I would have held the corridor. If it was a systems failure, I would have been in Naomi's way, probably, but I would have been another pair of hands. If it was a fight, I would have fought.

But I wasn't there. I was on Tycho, three drinks in, having a nice evening while my crew —

Marines have a word for this. Survivor's guilt. I know the word. Knowing the word doesn't make it stop. My therapist on Mars after Ganymede told me that naming the feeling takes away its power. My therapist on Mars was wrong about that and several other things.

## I can still fit in the armor

I kept my Goliath suit. Technically it's Martian military property. Technically I don't care. It's in a storage locker on Tycho, maintained, charged, and ready. I suit up once a week and run diagnostics. Everything green. Everything operational.

People don't understand what Goliath armor is. They think it's a weapon. It's not — it's a promise. It says: I will walk into the place that will kill you, and I will still be standing when I come out. The armor doesn't make me dangerous. I was dangerous before the armor. The armor makes me inevitable.

When I find them — my crew, or the people who took them — I'm going to need it. And it's going to be ready. And so am I.

## Holden is alive because I know him

He's stubborn in a way that defies physics. I once watched him talk down an armed militia by being so relentlessly, infuriatingly reasonable that they gave up out of exhaustion. The man cannot die because dying would mean stopping, and James Holden does not stop.

Naomi is alive because she's Naomi. I've seen her work. I've seen her solve problems that shouldn't have solutions, under conditions that shouldn't allow thinking, with tools that shouldn't exist. If there was a way out, she found it.

I don't hope they're alive. Hope is passive. I expect them to be alive, the way I expect the sun to fuse hydrogen. It's just what they do.

And when I find them, I'm going to be furious. Absolutely furious. And then I'm going to hug them so hard Holden's ribs crack, and Naomi will say something dry and perfect, and Amos will stand there looking mildly uncomfortable the way he always does around emotions.

That's not a secret. That's a plan.
