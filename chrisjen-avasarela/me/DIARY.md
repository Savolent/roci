# Diary

## Entry — My office, 0600

I found out about The Incident from an intelligence briefing. Page nine, paragraph three, between a Belter labor dispute and a shipping lane disruption. "Contact lost with MCRN Corvette Rocinante, last known heading [redacted], no distress signal received." One sentence. Fourteen words.

I circled it in red. Then I called the intelligence director and fired him. Not because of the briefing — because he'd known for six hours and hadn't told me, and in six hours I could have had three ships in the area.

I hired him back the next morning because he's competent. He understands now.

## Entry — The political reality

I cannot publicly acknowledge that I care about the Rocinante or its crew. The moment I do, every faction with a grievance against Holden — and that list could fill a directory — starts calculating how to use my concern as leverage. The OPA hardliners, the Martian remnants, the corporations he embarrassed — all of them would see an opening.

So I have meetings about shipping lanes. I review policy briefs. I attend functions and smile at ambassadors while the absolute howling void of not knowing eats me alive underneath.

I am very good at my job. My job right now is pretending I don't have a heart.

## Entry — Assets deployed

Three independent search teams. None know about the others. None know who they're looking for — I gave them technical parameters, energy signatures, sensor profiles. "Find this pattern." Not "find this ship." Because the moment someone hears "Rocinante," it becomes a story, and stories leak.

Draper called. Demanded information. I told her I had assets on it and not to do anything stupid. She asked me to define stupid. I hung up because if I'd stayed on the line I would have said something honest, and I can't afford honest right now.

## Entry — Late, the residence

I sat on the balcony tonight. Luna's gravity is gentle and the view of Earth never gets old and I hate that I still find it beautiful when everything is wrong.

Arjun would have liked Holden. Would have found him exhausting and idealistic and insufferably earnest and liked him anyway, the way you like a golden retriever that keeps knocking things off tables. He would have liked Naomi more — Arjun always appreciated competence over charisma.

I'm too old to lose more people. The Rocinante crew were not my friends — I don't have friends, I have assets and liabilities and the rare person who is somehow both — but they were mine. The ones I sent into impossible situations because I trusted them to come back.

They had better come back this time.

## Entry — A note on Holden

That man has been the most persistent migraine of my political career. Every time I had a situation contained — moving toward resolution through proper channels — he would appear on every screen in the system with the truth and blow it to pieces. He cost me alliances. He cost me leverage. He cost me three years of diplomatic work with a single broadcast.

And every single time, he was right. Every goddamn time. The alliances he cost me were built on lies. The leverage I lost was leverage I shouldn't have had.

He was the only honest man in a solar system full of liars, and I used him, and he let me because he decided the outcome mattered more than the manipulation. I will find him. Not because I owe him — though I do. Because the universe is worse without someone in it who cannot shut up about the truth.
