# Secrets

## I know more than I've told anyone

My intelligence people found something. Not the Roci — but an energy signature from eighteen hours before The Incident that shouldn't exist. Doesn't match any known weapon, drive technology, or natural phenomenon. Something new.

I've shown it to exactly two physicists, both with security clearances higher than most admirals. One said sensor error. The other went very quiet and asked where I got it. He said he needed a week. That was three weeks ago. He's stopped returning my calls.

I'm having him watched. Not because I don't trust him — because I do, and trusted people who go silent are the ones who've found something they're afraid of.

## The search is not off the books

Those assets I told Draper about are officially sanctioned — buried under seventeen layers of classification, authorized under an emergency directive I drafted and signed myself. If anyone finds out, it's a scandal. A sitting secretary-general using state intelligence resources to search for a private vessel.

I don't care. Fire me. I've been fired before. I came back, too, because they always need me. The people who want my job are welcome to it the moment the Rocinante crew is accounted for. Not one second before.

## I've been protecting Holden for years

He doesn't know. He thinks his broadcasts and his righteous truth-telling protected him. It didn't. He survived because every time some admiral or corporate board decided James Holden was more trouble than he was worth, I made a call. Sometimes a threat. Sometimes a favor. Once a very quiet conversation with a Martian intelligence officer who was planning an accident and who subsequently decided to retire.

I protected him because he was useful. That's what I told myself. But it wasn't the whole truth. I protected him because he was good. Actually, simply good, in a way that almost nobody in my world is. And the system needs that — at least one person who tells the truth because it's the truth and not because it's strategic.

I am strategic. Holden is good. It works better when we're both in it.

## The dream

Since the Roci disappeared, I dream about Arjun. He's young — six, maybe seven. Before he understood what I did for a living. But now he's looking up at the sky and pointing at something I can't see. He says: "They're not gone, Mama. You're just not looking in the right place."

I am a woman of politics and hard evidence. I do not make decisions based on dreams.

I reassigned two search teams based on that dream. One of them found the energy signature.

## I am tired

I'm seventy-three years old. My joints hurt. My doctor says my cortisol levels are "incompatible with continued service." I told him his employment was incompatible with giving me unsolicited opinions.

I should retire. Go back to Earth, sit in a garden, read books, eat food I didn't have to eat at a fundraiser. I've earned it.

But I can't stop yet. Not with the Roci missing. Not with Holden out there somewhere probably doing something stupid. Not with the rest of them scattered across the void, waiting for someone with the resources and the ruthlessness to find them.

After. I'll rest after. I've been saying that for forty years.
